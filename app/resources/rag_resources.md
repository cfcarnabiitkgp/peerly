CLARITY-FOCUSED Resources (70-90% clarity/communication)

  Highly Recommended for Clarity Agent:

  1. "How to Write Mathematics" by Paul Halmos (Classic, 1970/1973)
    - Core philosophy: "Aim for clarity, not pedantry"
    - Focus areas: Word choice, sentence structure, avoiding excessive symbolism
    - Key principle: "The best notation is no notation"
    - Emphasizes English style, grammar, punctuation
  2. "How to Write a Clear Math Paper" by Igor Pak (UCLA)
    - ~70-75% clarity-focused
    - Sections on: Structure, style, grammar, LaTeX presentation
    - Macro tips: titles, abstracts, introductions
    - Micro tips: punctuation, "style downshifting", figures
  3. "Practical Suggestions for Mathematical Writing" by Bjorn Poonen (MIT)
    - https://math.mit.edu/~poonen/papers/writing.pdf
    - Strong focus on readability and communication
  4. "Handbook of Writing for the Mathematical Sciences" by Nicholas Higham (SIAM)
    - Comprehensive: English usage, writing principles, TeX/LaTeX
    - Used as style reference by AMS and other publishers
    - Balances clarity with correctness

  ---
  RIGOR-FOCUSED Resources (50-70% rigor/validation)

  Highly Recommended for Rigor Agent:

  1. "Practical Suggestions for Mathematical Writing" by Bjorn Poonen (MIT)
    - https://math.mit.edu/~poonen/papers/writing.pdf
    - Proof structure and validation
  2. "Advice on Mathematical Writing" (UConn)
    - https://www2.math.uconn.edu/~khlee/math2784s10/writingtips.pdf
    - Focus on formal vs informal exposition
    - Proof validation guidelines
  3. AMS Style Guide & Author Handbook
    - Mathematical notation standards
    - Citation rigor and reference validation
    - Formal definitions and theorem structure

  BALANCED Resources (50/50 - Good for Both Agents)

  1. "A Few Tips on Writing Papers with Mathematical Content" by John Tsitsiklis (MIT)
    - https://www.mit.edu/~jnt/Papers/R-20-write-v5.pdf
    - Balances communication with correctness
  2. "Suggestions for Writing Mathematics" by Ruodu Wang (Waterloo)
    - https://sas.uwaterloo.ca/~wang/files/writing.pdf
    - Notation standards + communication principles
  3. "Writing a Mathematics Research Paper" (Fresno State/CURM)
    - https://zimmer.fresnostate.edu/~doreendl/uresearch/handouts/write_math_paper.pdf
